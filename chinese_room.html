Syntax vs. Semantics

Syntax: The rules and structure (what the person in the room follows)
Semantics: The meaning and understanding (what the person lacks)

Searle argues that computers, like the person in the room, only manipulate symbols according to rules. They have syntax but no semantics - no real understanding.

Can a system that perfectly simulates understanding
actually understand?
Or is it just following rules?

The Chinese Room argument challenges the idea that artificial intelligence can have genuine understanding or consciousness. It suggests that passing the Turing Test isn't enough to prove real intelligence. This remains one of the most debated topics in philosophy of mind and AI.
